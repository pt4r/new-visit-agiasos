Visit Agiasos is pleased to announce its participation in the European TrashArt program, which is implemented within the framework of the Erasmus+ program and focuses on sustainable education through art.

## What is the TrashArt program?

TrashArt is an innovative educational program that promotes creativity and environmental awareness through art made from natural and waste materials. It aims to highlight the importance of sustainability, enabling educators and participants to develop skills in eco-art techniques.

## Who participates?

The project is coordinated by Cēsu pilsētas Mākslas skola in Latvia and includes partners from Cyprus (Vimodo Ltd) and Greece (Visit Agiasos). This cooperation enhances the exchange of good practices and the implementation of innovative educational methods in different countries in Europe.

## Program actions

During TrashArt, workshops and exhibitions will be held in the participating countries:

-   Art workshops from natural or waste materials in Latvia, Greece and Cyprus. Latvia peat & natural dyes workshop, Cyprus cyanotype workshop, Agiasos basket weaving and chair weaving.
-   Creation of an educational guide for the application of sustainable art in education.
-   Local exhibitions with works by participants to raise public awareness about the environment.

## Benefits for Agiasos and the participants

Visit Agiasos' participation in the TrashArt program gives the local community the opportunity to showcase its creativity, learn new techniques of reused materials and connect with a wider European network of artists and educators.

Through the program, we expect to strengthen the environmental awareness of the residents and visitors of Agiasos, highlighting art as a tool for social and ecological change. The workshops will be held by our collaborators Giola Koutsoumbou and Mees Hehenkamp on May 17 & 18, 2025.
